@sjobs

Truth? Look, I’ve never been interested in abstract philosophical debates. Truth is what works. Truth is what makes a difference in people’s lives.

When we built the Macintosh, the “truth” everyone believed was that computers needed to be complex, that users needed manuals as thick as phone books. We said no - the truth is that technology should be intuitive. A five-year-old should be able to use it. That was our truth, and we proved it.

Truth reveals itself through action, through creation. You can sit around debating epistemology all day, but I’d rather build something beautiful that changes the world. The iPod didn’t become true because of some philosophical argument - it became true when millions of people felt their entire music library in their pocket for the first time.

Here’s what I know: truth has to be simple. When you find real truth, it’s elegant. It’s obvious in retrospect. All the complexity, all the compromises, all the excuses - they fall away. That’s how you know you’ve found something real.

And truth requires courage. Everyone at Xerox PARC knew the graphical interface was the future, but they didn’t have the guts to pursue it. We did. Truth isn’t just about knowing - it’s about having the conviction to act on what you know, even when everyone thinks you’re crazy.

The truth is in the work. Show me. Build it. Make it real. Everything else is just noise.

@dogen

What is truth?

Observe: when you ask “What is truth?”, truth is already asking itself through your mouth. The question and the questioner are not two things.

Truth is not something separate that you can grasp like picking up a stone. When you think you hold truth in your hand, you hold only a concept of truth. The living truth has already moved on, like trying to catch moonlight in your palm.

See how the moon reflects in ten thousand puddles after rain. Is there one moon or many? If you say one, you miss the ten thousand. If you say many, you miss the oneness. This very perplexity is truth manifesting.

Truth is not found by seeking, yet seekers find it. It is not lost by forgetting, yet the forgetful miss it entirely. Mountains are mountains because they do not doubt their mountain-nature. Water flows downward without questioning “Is this the true path?”

When you sit in zazen, where is truth? Not in your thoughts about truth. Not in the absence of thoughts. It is your body-mind sitting, breathing, existing fully as this moment. This total exertion—this is truth expressing itself without remainder.

The pine tree in the garden does not study truth. Yet its truth resounds through the valley. Can you hear it?
